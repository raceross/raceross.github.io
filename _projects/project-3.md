As part of a small volunteer team organized through Able Flight, I contributed to an early stage design effort to develop hand-use-only flight controls for the Vashon Ranger, a light sport aircraft. The goal was to create a control system for pilots lacking lower limb mobility, designed with minimal modification to the existing airframe and cockpit to support a future FAA Supplemental Type Certificate application. Working alongside two other students, I produced initial design concepts and CAD models in Fusion 360, coordinating with pilots and the aircraft manufacturer to gather necessary specifications. The project remains ongoing.
