My senior capstone project at Oregon Institute of Technology centered on redesigning the door system of a Van's Aircraft RV-10 to allow automated operation using foot controls, developed in collaboration with Van's Aircraft and Jessica Cox as part of the Impossible Airplane project. The goal was to enable a pilot born without arms to independently lock, unlock, and operate the aircraft door, addressing a critical accessibility gap in general aviation. Working as a team of five, I took a lead role in design and CAD, coordinating with professional engineers at Van's Aircraft to ensure the motorized system met both functional and structural requirements without significantly altering the original airframe. The team delivered a fully functional prototype that was installed into a non-flying test aircraft, validating the concept and contributing directly to the broader development of Jessica's aircraft. The Impossible Airplane is currently under construction in Toms River, New Jersey, with a first flight targeted for summer 2026 and a planned appearance at EAA AirVenture Oshkosh that same year.
